type classification
(collab project)

the nature of typography is inherently exclusive. the medium doesn’t call attention to itself, rather, retracting from the spotlight. good typography fades and lets the text speak for itself. as a result it’s accessibility is limited. aesthetic isolation can be broadly applied to design collateral e.g. type specimens. media designed for and by the same type of people has no capacity for evolution due to preexisting formulas that guarantee acceptance. attempting to combat this directly is fruitless, but alternative routes to gather content and a focus on process within a small collaborative environment yield atypical results.

in this book, people with little to no knowledge of type were interviewed which led to the exploration of misinformation.
