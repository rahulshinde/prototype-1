design has become stagnant. what was once an evolving form of visual communication has stopped changing. an industry that previously relied on others' ideas to dictate its methods of creation has forgotten the essence of design. design thinking/practice can no longer be a tool that is utilized by others. visual problem solving brings up possibilities that are to some extent absent from other methods of exploration. design allows for there to be visual representation of the problem, or solutions that stand apart from the issue. the output is not symbolic, it requires no explanation or prior knowledge. it can act as a visual experience, perhaps something closer to life.

the problem with already established mediums 1 and systems 2 is that they have inherent limitations. these aren’t defined by the user but instead by the physical or theoretical materials that make them up. to escape the cyclical nature of progress that ends in stagnation 3, we must first look at the root. it’s the tools used to execute that pose the real problem. for example, the creation of the internet has been the only medium that has come close to the idealistic entity 4 that i am imagining. while in theory the internet has an infinite webspace it’s indiscriminate storage of information allows only for expansion not evolution. on top of that, there is still a large population that doesn’t have access to this tool and, given the nature of tech-evolution5, will probably never have.

this is a student's perspective. one functioning in the system that is being denounced within this text. one who does not have the knowledge, resources, or a developed skill set to tackle the problems addressed above6. given the parameters it only seems acceptable to fully explore established mediums/methods of thinking. all studies contained on this site are simply that. they are meant to explore these mediums as defined by a) personal interest b) curriculum requirements.
